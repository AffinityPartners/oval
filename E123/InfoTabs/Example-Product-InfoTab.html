Product Overview

The EASE Group Fixed Indemnity Plan provides defined cash benefits to help offset common medical expenses from inpatient, outpatient, and emergency care. There is no lifetime maximum, and coverage includes hospital stays, surgeries, physician visits, and diagnostic services, all paid at fixed benefit amounts. Certain services such as emergency room visits, ambulance, and specialist visits have no waiting period, while facility-based care and pregnancy have applicable waiting times. The plan includes a 12-month pre-existing condition limitation, with exceptions for some office visits and imaging services.

Key Benefits Included:

  • Hospital Inpatient Confinement: $1,500 per day, up to 10 days
  • ICU Confinement: $2,000 per day, up to 10 days
  • Inpatient Surgery (Physician): $750 per admit, up to 3 admits
  • Inpatient Surgery (Anesthesia): $300 per admit, up to 3 admits
  • Outpatient Surgery (Facility): $2,000 per admit, up to 3 admits
  • Outpatient Surgery (Physician): $500 per admit, up to 3 admits
  • Outpatient Surgery (Anesthesia): $300 per admit, up to 3 admits
  • Emergency Room: $500 per visit, up to 2 visits
  • Ambulance: $250 per ride, 1 per year
  • Physician or Specialist Office Visit: $75 per visit, up to 5 visits
  • Radiology/Imaging Service Facility: $100 per visit, 1 per year

Important Plan Limitations

Simultaneous Benefits:

Plan will not pay hospital inpatient confinement benefit and the hospital ICU confinement benefit simultaneously. If both inpatient and ICU statuses occur on the same day, the higher benefit between the two would be paid for that day.

Emergency Room vs. Inpatient:

Plan will not pay hospital inpatient confinement benefit and emergency room benefit simultaneously, unless the services within the emergency room setting lasted more than eighteen (18) hours.

Observation Hours:

Observation hours are not typically considered inpatient and are not included in the Inpatient Confinement benefit. However, if the observation hours extend beyond eighteen (18) hours during an emergency room visit, and the member is then admitted as an inpatient confinement, both the observation hours (after the 18th hour) and the inpatient stay would be covered by the Inpatient Confinement benefit.
