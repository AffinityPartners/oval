Important GLP-1 Program Information

Eligibility & Approval

Injectable GLP-1 medications require specialist approval and are not appropriate for everyone. Eligibility is based on BMI, medical history, current health conditions, contraindications, and clinical protocols. Common reasons for denial include pregnancy/nursing, history of thyroid cancer, severe gastrointestinal disease, or certain other medical conditions. Your specialist will discuss alternative options if GLP-1s are not appropriate for you.

Common Side Effects

GLP-1 medications commonly cause gastrointestinal side effects, especially during dose increases. These may include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain, and decreased appetite. Most side effects are mild to moderate and improve with time. Serious side effects are rare but can include pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, kidney problems, and severe allergic reactions. Report all side effects to your provider immediately.

Treatment Duration & Cost

GLP-1 treatment is typically long-term (12+ months) for optimal results and weight maintenance. Member-exclusive pricing is applied to all prescriptions, but medication costs are separate from your membership fee. Your concierge will discuss pricing options during onboarding. Most patients require ongoing treatment to maintain weight loss; discontinuation often leads to weight regain.

Injection Administration

GLP-1 medications are self-administered via subcutaneous injection (typically in the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm) once weekly. Training resources and injection supplies are provided. Most patients find injections easy and painless after the first few doses. Your concierge is available to answer any questions about injection technique.

Lifestyle Requirements

GLP-1 medications are NOT a substitute for healthy lifestyle habits. Maximum results require commitment to improved nutrition, regular physical activity, adequate hydration, and behavioral changes. Your program includes coaching and support, but your effort and adherence determine your success.

Not for Cosmetic Weight Loss

GLP-1 medications are approved for patients with obesity (BMI ≥30) or overweight (BMI ≥27) with weight-related health conditions. These are not appropriate for cosmetic weight loss in individuals at healthy weights. Your specialist will determine if you meet clinical criteria based on your assessment.
